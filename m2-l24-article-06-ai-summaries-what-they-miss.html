AI-Generated Summaries in Healthcare: What They Get Right, What They Miss, and Why You Can't Just Trust Them

AI can generate a patient summary in seconds. That speed is real and valuable. But the patterns of failure — the specific ways AI summaries fall short — are predictable and teachable. Know the failure modes and you'll never be caught off guard by them.

What AI Gets Right — and Where the Problems Start

✅ What AI summaries genuinely do well
Processing large volumes of clinical notes quickly
Organizing information into consistent structures
Extracting medication lists from unstructured notes
Creating a readable first draft of standard elements
Catching items mentioned across multiple notes
Reducing transcription time significantly
❌ Where AI summaries consistently fail
Presenting uncertain information as definite fact
Omitting context that changes meaning entirely
Missing significance of abnormal values
Flattening nuanced clinical thinking into simple statements
Failing to flag information outside the record it accessed
Missing clinically significant trends (improving vs. worsening)

What to Remember

Five specific AI summary failure modes: presenting uncertainty as certainty, omitting context that changes meaning, missing the significance of abnormal values, flattening clinical nuance, and missing trends (only seeing the most recent value without the trajectory).

AI reports what it found in the data without judgment about what it means. A normal-looking current value may represent dangerous improvement from a critical baseline. A condition labeled "stable" may be the AI's interpretation of "not mentioned as unstable." Context and clinical significance require human judgment.

The review standard for AI summaries: don't just check if the values are correct — check whether the summary tells the right clinical story. A summary can have accurate facts and still mislead a provider if critical context, trends, or uncertainty signals are absent.
