Lesson 2.3 · Editing AI Outputs

The 5-Minute AI Edit: A Practical Checklist for Reviewing Healthcare Documents Before You Send

The video tells you to review AI output before sending. This article gives you the exact repeatable process — five steps, five minutes, every time. Once you internalize this workflow, reviewing AI-generated healthcare documents becomes fast, thorough, and second nature.

Why You Need a Repeatable Process

The biggest risk with AI-assisted documentation isn't that people skip the review entirely. It's that they review inconsistently — scanning quickly when they're busy, being more careful when they have time. The problem is that errors don't know when you're busy. A repeatable checklist removes inconsistency from the equation. You run the same five steps every single time, regardless of how rushed you feel.

Five minutes sounds short, but for most routine healthcare documents — a staff email, a patient letter, a shift memo — it's enough time to catch every category of AI error that matters. The key is working through the steps in order, not jumping around based on what catches your eye.

A review that's always the same is worth more than a review that's sometimes thorough. Consistency is the safety net.

The 5-Step AI Edit Checklist

Work through these steps in order every time you review an AI-generated healthcare document. Each step takes roughly one minute for most routine documents:

1
~1 min
Read the whole thing before changing anything
Resist the urge to start editing on line one. Read the entire document from start to finish — as if you're the reader receiving it, not the writer reviewing it. This gives you the full picture before you start making changes.
📌 While reading, ask: Does this make sense as a whole? Does anything feel off, missing, or wrong? Take a mental note — don't edit yet.
2
~1 min
Verify every specific fact
Go line by line and check every specific — every name, date, medication, dosage, number, time, and clinical detail. These are the highest-risk elements in any healthcare document because AI can get them wrong with perfect confidence.
🔴 Check: Patient names and identifiers · Dates and times · Medication names, doses, and frequencies · Procedure names · Provider names · Numbers and statistics
3
~1 min
Check completeness — what's missing?
AI produces documents that sound complete but frequently omit critical information. After verifying facts, ask: Is everything that needs to be here actually here? Look specifically for missing action items, missing follow-up timelines, and missing responsible parties.
🟠 Ask for each section: Does the reader have everything they need to act on this? Is there a specific next step? Is there a named person responsible? Is there a deadline?
4
~1 min
Check tone for your audience
Re-read the document through the eyes of the intended reader. For patient-facing documents: is it warm and clear? For clinical notes: is it precise and objective? For internal memos: is it professional and appropriately direct? AI frequently gets the tone slightly wrong for the specific audience.
🟣 Ask: Would the intended reader feel respected by this document? Does anything sound cold, dismissive, overly clinical, or inappropriately casual?
5
~1 min
Final read-aloud (or slow read)
Read the document aloud — or as slowly as if you're reading it for the first time and every word matters. This catches awkward phrasing, run-on sentences, anything that doesn't quite sound right, and the small errors that eyes tend to skip over when reading at normal speed.
✅ If everything sounds right spoken aloud, the document is ready. If you stumble on a sentence, that sentence needs to be rewritten.

📌 Article Summary

What to Remember

The 5-step AI edit checklist: (1) Read the whole document before changing anything. (2) Verify every specific fact. (3) Check completeness — what's missing? (4) Check tone for your audience. (5) Final read-aloud. Run these in order, every time, for every AI-generated document.

The most dangerous review habit is inconsistency — being thorough when you have time, scanning when you're busy. A repeatable checklist eliminates that inconsistency. Errors don't know when you're having a hectic day.

Step 2 — verifying every specific fact — is the highest-stakes step for healthcare documents. Names, dates, medications, dosages, and numbers are where AI errors cause the most harm if missed.
