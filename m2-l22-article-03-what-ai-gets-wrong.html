AI Writes Confidently — Even When It's Wrong

The most dangerous thing about AI documentation errors isn't that they're obvious. It's that they're not. AI writes in fluent, professional-sounding language whether it's correct or completely wrong. A note that says "the patient was given lisinopril 100mg" reads with exactly the same confidence as one that says "the patient was given lisinopril 10mg" — even though the first dose is ten times too high and potentially fatal.

Confidence of language is not evidence of accuracy. This is the single most important thing to internalize before using any AI tool for healthcare documentation. The review step isn't a formality — it's the safety net that makes AI-assisted documentation safe.

AI doesn't know the difference between something it made up and something that's true. It can't. That's your job.

What to Remember

AI writes with equal confidence whether correct or wrong. The 5 most common AI drafting failures in healthcare: hallucination (inventing details), tone mismatch, missing context, false certainty about uncertain information, and wrong format for the setting.

Specific numbers, dates, names, and medical facts are the highest-risk elements in any AI-generated healthcare document — these must always be verified against the original source before any document is sent or signed.

Every AI draft review should ask: Did the AI add anything I didn't provide? Is every specific fact in here something I can verify? Is the tone right for who will actually read this? Does the format match the setting and document type?
