Lesson 2.2 · Using AI to Draft Documentation

Before AI, After AI: Real Before-and-After Examples of Healthcare Documents

The best way to see the value — and the limits — of AI-assisted drafting is side by side. These comparisons show raw human-only drafts, unedited AI drafts, and the final human-edited versions together so you can see exactly what each contributes.

What the Three-Version Comparison Shows You

Each comparison in this article shows three versions of the same document: the original human-only draft (how it was written before AI tools were available), the unedited AI draft (what AI produces from a well-written prompt), and the final human-edited version (what gets sent after the professional reviews and improves the AI output).

What you'll see: AI is consistently better than rushed human drafts on structure and language. But the final edited version is always better than the unedited AI draft — because the human catches what the AI got wrong and adds what the AI couldn't know. That's the partnership.

📌 Article Summary

What to Remember

AI drafts consistently outperform rushed human drafts on structure, professional language, and completeness — making them a genuinely useful starting point for most routine healthcare documentation tasks.

The final human-edited version is always better than the raw AI draft — not because humans are better at formatting, but because humans add specific facts, contextual accuracy, appropriate tone calibration, and catch the errors AI introduced.

The three-version pattern — human raw draft, AI draft, final edited version — is the most honest way to see what AI actually contributes to the writing process and where human expertise remains essential.
