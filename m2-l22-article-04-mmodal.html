Real-World Spotlight: How 3M M*Modal Uses AI to Draft Clinical Summaries

3M M*Modal is one of the leading AI-powered clinical documentation systems used by health systems across the US. Understanding how it actually works — and what role humans play in the process — gives you a concrete mental model for AI-assisted drafting in any healthcare setting.

Who Is 3M M*Modal?

3M M*Modal (acquired by 3M Health Information Systems and now part of Solventum after a spin-off) is a company that has been building clinical documentation technology for over 20 years. Their flagship product, M*Modal Fluency Direct, is an AI-powered voice-to-text system used by physicians and clinical staff to dictate and draft medical documentation — directly inside EHR systems like Epic and Cerner.

Unlike general AI writing tools, M*Modal was trained specifically on clinical language from over 100 million medical documents. It understands medical terminology, clinical note structure, specialty-specific language, and even individual physician dictation patterns. When a cardiologist says "the patient had an NSTEMI with a peak troponin of 4.2 nanograms per milliliter," M*Modal knows exactly how to format that in a clinical note.

The M*Modal Workflow: Before and After Implementation

Here's what documentation workflows typically look like at health systems before and after implementing M*Modal:

📋 Before M*Modal
Physicians type notes after patient encounters — often hours later
Documentation happens at desktop workstations, not at point of care
Notes often completed at home ("pajama time") after shifts
Human transcriptionists review and format dictated notes
24–48 hour lag between encounter and finalized note
Note quality varies significantly by physician typing speed
✅ After M*Modal
Physicians dictate in real time during or immediately after the patient encounter
Notes appear structured in the EHR within seconds of dictation
Documentation largely complete before leaving the exam room
AI replaces transcriptionist for routine cases; human review for complex ones
Notes available to the full care team almost immediately
Note completeness improved by AI prompts for missing clinical elements

The Human Review Step: What Physicians Still Do

Despite M*Modal's capabilities, health systems using it are explicit: the physician's review of every AI-generated note is non-negotiable. Here's what the human review step actually looks like in practice:

✅
Verify every medication name, dose, and frequency
M*Modal is highly accurate with common medications but can mishear brand vs. generic names, confuse similar-sounding drugs, or capture dictated doses incorrectly. Every medication line in an AI draft requires a human verification check.
🔍
Add context the AI couldn't capture
M*Modal processes speech — it cannot see that the patient was crying during the conversation about their diagnosis, or that the family member in the room reacted with alarm, or that the physician examined a specific rash that wasn't verbally described. These contextual details must be added manually.
⚠️
Correct ambiguity resolutions the AI got wrong
When a dictated sentence is ambiguous ("the patient takes her medication twice" — twice what?), the AI makes a judgment call. Sometimes it's right. Sometimes it fills in with a plausible but incorrect interpretation. Review identifies these and corrects them before the note enters the record.
📋
Confirm the assessment and plan match the encounter
The Assessment and Plan section — where the physician documents their clinical reasoning and treatment decisions — requires the most careful review. AI can draft the structure but cannot verify that the logic matches what actually happened in the room.

M*Modal doesn't replace clinical judgment — it eliminates the friction between what a physician thinks and what ends up in the chart. The thinking is still entirely human.

📌 Article Summary

What to Remember

3M M*Modal is an AI-powered clinical documentation system trained on 100+ million clinical documents. It converts physician dictation into structured EHR notes, assists with clinical coding, and flags documentation gaps — reducing documentation time by approximately 40%.

Even with specialized clinical AI training, M*Modal requires physician review of every note — particularly for medication details, ambiguity resolutions, contextual information not captured in speech, and the accuracy of the assessment and plan section.

The signature step is the accountability step — regardless of which AI tool generated the note, signing it makes the physician legally and professionally responsible for every word. No AI company accepts that responsibility. The human always does.
