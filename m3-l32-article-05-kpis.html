Key Performance Indicators in Healthcare: The 12 Numbers Every Healthcare Worker Should Be Able to Read

Dashboards are only useful when you know what "good" looks like. This article gives you the 12 most important healthcare operational KPIs — what each measures, the target ranges, the red flags, and what action each one calls for.

Why Every Healthcare Worker Needs to Know These Numbers

You can sit in front of a dashboard all day and not learn anything if you don't know what the numbers mean. A bed occupancy of 87% means nothing without knowing that the safe operating range is 80–85% and that anything over 92% is a capacity crisis. A nurse-to-patient ratio of 1:5 means nothing without knowing that safe limits are typically 1:4 or lower.

Context turns numbers into information. These 12 KPIs are the context layer for every healthcare operations dashboard you'll ever encounter. Memorize the general ranges. The specific targets will vary by facility and unit type — but the framework stays the same.

📌 Key Takeaways

What to Remember From This Article

KPI knowledge is the context layer that turns dashboard numbers into information. Without knowing what "good" looks like for each metric, a dashboard is just a screen full of numbers.

The 12 core KPIs span four categories: patient flow (occupancy, ER wait time, LOS, no-show rate), staffing (nurse ratio, utilization, overtime, turnover), quality (medication accuracy, satisfaction, readmissions), and operations (supply fill rate, revenue cycle). Together they give a complete operational picture.

Specific targets vary by facility type, unit, and state regulations — but the general ranges in this article give you a reliable baseline for evaluating any healthcare operations dashboard.
