Lesson 2.2 · Using AI to Draft Documentation

The Prompt Is Everything: How to Give AI Clear Instructions for Healthcare Writing

The quality of what AI produces is almost entirely determined by the quality of what you ask for. A vague prompt produces a generic draft. A specific, context-rich prompt produces something you can actually use. This article teaches you exactly how to write one.

Why Prompting Is a Skill

Think of an AI writing tool like a very capable assistant who just joined your team. They're smart, fast, and willing to help — but they know nothing about your workplace, your patients, your documentation standards, or the specific situation you're dealing with. If you walk up to them and say "write an email," they'll write something generic and technically correct but completely useless for your actual situation.

Now imagine you give that assistant every piece of relevant context: who the email is for, what it needs to say, what tone is appropriate, how long it should be, and what specific action you need the reader to take. Suddenly, the assistant can produce something that actually works. That's prompting. The difference between a great AI output and a useless one is usually the difference between a great prompt and a bad one.

You are not describing what you want. You are writing instructions for a very literal, very capable assistant who will do exactly what you say — and only what you say.

Weak Prompt vs. Strong Prompt: Side by Side

The same task — writing a patient follow-up reminder — produces completely different results depending on how the prompt is written:

❌ Weak Prompt
"Write a reminder for patients about their follow-up appointment."
AI Output (Typical Result)
"Dear Patient,\nThis is a reminder that you have an upcoming follow-up appointment. Please contact our office to confirm or reschedule.\nThank you,\n[Clinic Name]"
Why it fails: Generic template with zero patient-specific information. No appointment date, no clinic name, no phone number, no instructions. Needs to be entirely rewritten.
✅ Strong Prompt
"Write a friendly patient reminder letter for a follow-up appointment at Green Valley Family Clinic. Appointment is Tuesday June 25 at 2:30pm with Dr. Reyes. Patient should arrive 10 minutes early and bring their updated medication list. Include our number (555-204-1100) to reschedule. Warm but professional tone. 100 words max."
AI Output (Typical Result)
"Dear [Patient Name],\nThis is a friendly reminder of your follow-up appointment with Dr. Reyes at Green Valley Family Clinic on Tuesday, June 25th at 2:30pm.\nPlease plan to arrive 10 minutes early and bring an updated list of your current medications.\nIf you need to reschedule, please call us at (555) 204-1100.\nWe look forward to seeing you!\nGreen Valley Family Clinic"
Why it works: All key facts included, correct contact info, appropriate warm tone, ready to use with only the patient name to add.

📌 Article Summary

What to Remember

A strong healthcare prompt has six components: document type, audience, key facts, tone, constraint (length/format), and action (what the document needs to achieve). Missing any of these produces a weaker, more generic output that requires more editing.

The gap between a weak prompt and a strong prompt is the difference between a generic template you have to rewrite and a working draft you only need to refine. Investing 60 seconds in a better prompt saves 10 minutes of editing.

Never include real patient information in public AI tool prompts — use descriptive placeholders instead (e.g., "a patient with diabetes and hypertension" not a real name and DOB). This is both an ethical responsibility and a HIPAA compliance requirement.
