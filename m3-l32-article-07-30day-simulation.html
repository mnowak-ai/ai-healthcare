📌 What This Activity Tested

The Skills You Just Used

Trend identification: Reading 30 days of data to find which metrics have been moving consistently in the wrong direction — not just reacting to today's numbers.

Root cause thinking: Recognizing that ER wait times, bed occupancy, nurse ratios, and satisfaction don't always have four separate causes — they can all connect back to one. Finding that connection is what separates a data-literate healthcare worker from one who just reads numbers.

Action translation: Moving from "the data says this" to "we should do this" — the critical last step that most dashboards cannot do for you. This is the human judgment layer, and it's what this entire lesson has been building toward.
