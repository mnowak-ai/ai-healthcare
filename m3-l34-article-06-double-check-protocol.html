The Double-Check Protocol: How to Verify AI Recommendations Against Real Data Before Acting

Verification is a skill, not an attitude. This article turns "always double-check" into a specific, step-by-step protocol you can apply to any AI recommendation in healthcare operations — and includes an interactive checklist you can actually use in the field.

The Problem With "Just Double-Check It"

Healthcare workers are often told to verify AI outputs before acting on them. What they're rarely told is exactly what that means operationally. "Double-check it" sounds clear — but what are you checking? Against what? Using what sources? In what order? How long should it take? What do you do when the check raises a concern?

Without answers to these questions, "verify the AI output" becomes a vague instruction that different people interpret differently — and some interpret as "scan it quickly and move on." The Double-Check Protocol transforms verification from a vague standard into a repeatable, specific, learnable skill.

📌 Key Takeaways

What to Remember From This Article

The four protocol steps: (1) Apply the three verification questions, (2) Compare with real current data, (3) Review relevant policies, (4) Escalate or document. In order, every time. The sequence matters — real data comes before policy review because data inconsistencies often surface concerns that change what you bring to a policy check.

"Compare with real data" is specific, not general. For staffing: compare predicted census to actual current census and confirmed admissions/discharges. For scheduling: compare predicted fill rate to actual booked appointments in real time. For supply: compare predicted days-remaining to actual confirmed usage patterns for this specific period.

Documentation is part of the protocol, not an afterthought. The record that you verified — and what you found — is what demonstrates professional judgment and creates the audit trail that helps identify systematic AI errors over time.
