The Three Documents Every Healthcare Worker Writes

Emails. Memos. Reports. These three document types show up in every healthcare role — from front desk to clinical support to management. Understanding what each one is, what it must contain, and what it absolutely cannot contain is foundational to working in healthcare.

Why These Three Documents?

Healthcare workers write many types of documents over their careers — clinical notes, incident reports, referral letters, discharge summaries. But three types appear in virtually every role, every day, across every type of healthcare setting: the professional email, the internal memo, and the shift or status report.

Getting these three right is the foundation of everything else. If you can write a clear, professional email, a well-structured memo, and an accurate report, you already have the skills that most healthcare roles actually need from you day to day. Everything else builds from here.

📌 Article Summary

What to Remember

Professional emails are for direct communication between people. They need a clear subject, professional greeting, single clear purpose, and a specific call to action. Never include patient info in unsecured email.

Internal memos are for official communications — policy changes, announcements, formal requests. They need a header (To/From/Date/Re), a clear opening that states the purpose, and specific action items with dates.

Reports (including shift summaries, status updates, and clinical reports) must be objective, factual, and complete. They document what happened — not what you think happened or how you felt about it.
